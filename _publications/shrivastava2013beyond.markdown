We go beyond the notion of pairwise similarity and look into search problems with \(k\)-way similarity functions. In this paper, we focus on problems related to \emph&amp;\#123;3-way Jaccard&amp;\#125; similarity: \(\mathcal\&amp;\#123;R\&amp;\#125;^\&amp;\#123;3way\&amp;\#125;= \frac\&amp;\#123;|S\_1 \cap S\_2 \cap S\_3|\&amp;\#125;\&amp;\#123;|S\_1 \cup S\_2 \cup S\_3|\&amp;\#125;\), \(S\_1, S\_2, S\_3 \in \mathcal\&amp;\#123;C\&amp;\#125;\), where \(\mathcal\&amp;\#123;C\&amp;\#125;\) is a size \(n\) collection of sets (or binary vectors). We show that approximate \(\mathcal\&amp;\#123;R\&amp;\#125;^\&amp;\#123;3way\&amp;\#125;\) similarity search problems admit fast algorithms with provable guarantees, analogous to the pairwise case. Our analysis and speedup guarantees naturally extend to \(k\)-way resemblance. In the process, we extend traditional framework of \emph&amp;\#123;locality sensitive hashing (LSH)&amp;\#125; to handle higher order similarities, which could be of independent theoretical interest. The applicability of \(\mathcal\&amp;\#123;R\&amp;\#125;^\&amp;\#123;3way\&amp;\#125;\) search is shown on the Google sets application. In addition, we demonstrate the advantage of \(\mathcal\&amp;\#123;R\&amp;\#125;^\&amp;\#123;3way\&amp;\#125;\) resemblance over the pairwise case in improving retrieval quality.
