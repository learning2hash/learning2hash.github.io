We give an example of an invariant bond percolation process on the slab
\(\mathbb\{Z\}^2\times \\{0,1\\}\) with the property that it has infinitely many
clusters whose critical percolation probability is strictly less than \(1\). We
also show that no such process can exist in \(\mathbb\{Z\}^2\).
