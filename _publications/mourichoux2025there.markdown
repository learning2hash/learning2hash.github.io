A point of a metric space is called a \(k\)-hub if it is the endpoint of
exactly \(k\) disjoint geodesics, and that the concatenation of any two of these
paths is still a geodesic. We prove that in the Brownian sphere, there is no
\(k\)-hub for \(k\geq 3\).
