Fast Algorithms For Exact String Matching

Srikrishnan Divakaran . Arxiv 2015 – 1 citation

[Paper]    

Given a pattern string (P) of length (n) and a query string (T) of length (m), where the characters of (P) and (T) are drawn from an alphabet of size (\Delta), the {\em exact string matching} problem consists of finding all occurrences of (P) in (T). For this problem, we present algorithms that in (O(n\Delta^2)) time pre-process (P) to essentially identify (sparse(P)), a rarely occurring substring of (P), and then use it to find occurrences of (P) in (T) efficiently. Our algorithms require a worst case search time of (O(m)), and expected search time of (O(m/min(|sparse(P)|, \Delta))), where (|sparse(P)|) is at least (\delta) (i.e. the number of distinct characters in (P)), and for most pattern strings it is observed to be (Ω(n^{1/2})).
